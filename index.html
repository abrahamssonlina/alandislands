The Åland Islands

The island state with endless opportunities

This is Åland

The Åland Islands are a unique group of islands and islets in the middle of the Baltic Sea. Its archipelago consists of around 6 700 islands and is a self-autonomous part of Finland. Despite its mere 30 000 inhabitants Åland offers a rich culture life, long-lived traditions and many events, especially during the summer.

Warm cliffs in red granite, the sun that sparkles in the sea and lots of good food is a few key components in the classic picture of these islands. Keep on reading to get a deeper look into what Åland has to offer!

When to visit
The climate has its peak during July and is considered to be the high season. Many of the island's events also take place during this month.
How to get here
There are numerous ferries and cruise ships in traffic between Stockholm, Helsinki, Turku and the Åland Islands, eg. Viking Line, Tallink Silja, and Birka Cruises.
